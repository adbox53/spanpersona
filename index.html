Jordan – The Consistency Rebuilder

Jordan wants to stay active long-term without triggering setbacks. They value clarity, simplicity, and confidence in daily movement decisions.

CORE PROFILE

  • Age: 45
  • Primary Roles: Working professional, household contributor, self-managed health decision-maker
  • Behavior Pattern: Weekend push → soreness → skipped days → restart cycle

GOALS

  • Build a durable movement habit
  • Avoid multi-day setbacks
  • Maintain mobility and independence
  • Understand why today’s plan changes
  • Stay consistent without pressure

KEY TASKS IN SPAN

  • Complete ≤60 second daily check-in
  • Understand Risk vs Goals Score
  • Choose one recommended daily action
  • Track weekly momentum
  • Optional movement screen

PAIN POINTS

  • Time scarcity
  • Overly complex dashboards
  • Fear of injury
  • Confusion about soreness
  • Feeling “behind” after missing a day

MENTAL MODEL (CRITICAL FOR DESIGN)

Risk Score: “How likely I am to have a setback if I push today.”

Goals Score: “How consistent I’ve been this week.”

Design Target: ≥80% of users can explain the difference without help.

USABILITY & SUCCESS METRICS

  • SUS ≥ 75
  • NASA-TLX Mental Demand < 25
  • NASA-TLX Temporal Demand < 25
  • Daily interaction ≤ 60 seconds
  • Max 3 plan cards
  • ≥60% complete low-risk plan on high-risk days
Jordan does not need optimization. Jordan needs clear, low-friction guidance that protects consistency.
